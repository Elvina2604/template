Dogs

Scientists have discovered that golden retrievers have the secret to longevity

Researchers from the American California State University have found that golden retrievers may hold the secret to longevity. It is noted that this breed of dog has a 65% chance of dying from cancer, but the exact reason for this is unknown. Writing in GeroScience , the scientists said they were tasked with identifying what genetic factors might increase the survival rate of these dogs and discovered that they had a gene associated with longevity.

A farmer accidentally caught an animal that was thought to be extinct

Farmer Pao Ling Tsai from Beachport, South Australia, set up a trap on his own farm because he wanted to catch the animal that ate his chickens.

Imagine his surprise when, instead of a cat or fox, he saw a spotted-tailed marsupial marten, widely found throughout the state in the 1880s, and now under threat of extinction throughout the planet.

Intresting facts

There’s no doubt we love our pets and in all their many shapes and sizes. Here’s some fun and amazing facts about our beloved pets

There are more than 350 different breeds of dogs worldwide.

Domesticated for more than 10,000 years, the dog was one of the first animals domesticated by humans.

Three dogs survived the Titanic sinking.

Dogs have three eyelids.

Dogs are as smart as a 2 year-old.

Cats spend 30% of their waking hours grooming themselves.

Cats have better memories than dogs. Tests conducted by the University of Michigan concluded that while a dog’s memory lasts no more than 5 minutes, a cat’s can last as long as 16 hours—exceeding even that of monkeys and orangutans.
